## The Science of Effective Subtitles

> **"Great subtitles are invisible‚Äîthey enhance without distracting."**

### Readability Fundamentals

**Font Choice:**
* **Sans-serif fonts** for maximum legibility
* **Bold or semi-bold weight** for visibility
* **Avoid decorative fonts** that slow reading
* **Consistent font** throughout video

**Popular choices:** Montserrat Bold, Helvetica Bold, Arial Bold

**Size Matters:**
* **Mobile-first sizing:** Readable on smallest screens
* **10-15% of video height** is optimal
* **Test on actual device** before publishing
* **Bigger is better** than too small

**Color and Contrast:**
* **White text with black outline** is the gold standard
* **High contrast** ensures visibility on any background
* **Avoid low-contrast combinations** (gray on white, etc.)
* **Brand colors** can work if contrast is maintained

---

### Timing and Pacing

**Segment Length:**
* **Maximum 2 lines** per subtitle segment
* **8-12 words per segment** for comfortable reading
* **Complete thoughts** in each segment
* **No mid-sentence cuts** when possible

**Display Duration:**
* **Minimum 1 second** per segment
* **Maximum 7 seconds** before changing
* **Match natural speech rhythm**
* **Sync perfectly** with audio

**Reading Speed:**
* **Average adult reads 250 words/minute**
* **On video, aim for 200 words/minute** max
* **Slower for complex topics**
* **Faster for familiar content**

**The Rule:** Viewers should easily read the full subtitle before it disappears.

* Speaker 1: Bottom center

* Speaker 3: Top left

**Why it works:** Prevents confusion, maintains flow, builds character recognition

## Common Subtitle Mistakes

### Mistake #1: Auto-Generated Without Review

**The problem:**
* AI mishears technical terms
* Homophone errors (their/there/they're)
* Missing punctuation
* Nonsensical segments

**The solution:**
* **Always review** auto-generated subtitles
* **Edit for accuracy** before publishing
* **Add punctuation** for clarity
* **Fix technical terms** manually

---

### Mistake #2: Text Over Important Visuals

**The problem:**
* Covers faces during reactions
* Hides on-screen graphics
* Obscures product features
* Blocks important UI elements

**The solution:**
* **Dynamic positioning** based on frame content
* **Semi-transparent backgrounds** when over visuals
* **Move text location** as needed
* **Plan shot composition** with subtitles in mind

---

### Mistake #3: Inconsistent Styling

**The problem:**
* Font changes mid-video
* Color shifts without reason
* Size variations randomly
* Different animation styles

**The solution:**
* **Create style guide** for your subtitles
* **Use templates** for consistency
* **Batch process** with same settings
* **Brand consistency** across all videos

---

### Mistake #4: Too Much Text

**The problem:**
* Viewers can't read fast enough
* Screen feels cluttered
* Overwhelms rather than assists
* Reduces retention

**The solution:**
* **Condense text** to key points
* **Remove filler words** ("um," "like")
* **Break into smaller segments**
* **Prioritize clarity** over completeness

---

### Mistake #5: Poor Contrast

**The problem:**
* White text on light backgrounds
* Colored text without outlines
* Transparent or faded text
* Unable to read on mobile

**The solution:**
* **Always use outlines** or backgrounds
* **Test on multiple backgrounds**
* **Check on mobile device**
* **High contrast** is non-negotiable
