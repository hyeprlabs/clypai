## Building Your System: The Foundation

### Step 1: Choose Your Storage Architecture

**Cloud-Based Options:**

**Google Drive / Dropbox:**
* **Pros:** Accessible anywhere, easy sharing, good search
* **Cons:** Storage costs, upload/download times
* **Best for:** Collaborative teams, multi-device access

**Dedicated Video Platforms:**
* **Pros:** Built for video, transcoding included, streaming capable
* **Cons:** Higher costs, platform lock-in
* **Best for:** Large video libraries, professional workflows

**Local + Backup Hybrid:**
* **Pros:** Fast access, lower ongoing costs, full control
* **Cons:** Hardware dependency, requires backup discipline
* **Best for:** Solo creators, cost-conscious workflows

**Recommendation:** Hybrid approach with local working storage + cloud backup

---

### Step 2: Establish Naming Conventions

**Consistent naming is non-negotiable.**

**File naming template:**
```
YYYY-MM-DD_ContentType_Title_Version
```

**Examples:**
```
2026-02-13_Podcast_AI-Tools-Interview_FINAL
2026-02-13_Stream_Valorant-Ranked_VOD
2026-02-13_Clip_Epic-Clutch_TikTok-v2
```

**Why this works:**
* **Chronological sorting** happens automatically
* **Type identification** at a glance
* **Version control** prevents confusion
* **Searchable** by any component

## The Repurposing Workflow

### From Library to Published Content

**Weekly repurposing session:**

**1. Review inventory (15 minutes)**
* Check performance data
* Identify underutilized content
* Spot trending topics in archives

**2. Select content (10 minutes)**
* Choose based on current trends
* Match to upcoming content calendar
* Prioritize high-quality moments

**3. Extract and adapt (30 minutes)**
* Pull relevant clips
* Reformat for target platform
* Update context if needed

**4. Schedule and publish (15 minutes)**
* Add to content calendar
* Schedule across platforms
* Update metadata as "used"

**Total time:** 70 minutes for week of repurposed content
