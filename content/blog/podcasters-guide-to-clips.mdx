## The 5-Clip Framework

Every podcast episode should generate at least 5 clips. Here's the formula:

### 1. The Hook Clip (15-30 seconds)
The most provocative or intriguing moment. This is pure bait designed to stop scrolls and spark curiosity.

**Example**: *"I lost $2 million before I learned this one lesson..."*

### 2. The Value Bomb (30-60 seconds)
A complete, actionable insight that delivers immediate value. Viewers should feel smarter after watching.

**Example**: Framework explanations, tactical tips, counterintuitive advice

### 3. The Controversy Clip (30-45 seconds)
A hot take or polarizing opinion that generates discussion in the comments.

**Example**: *"Most productivity advice is actually making you less productive..."*

### 4. The Story Moment (60-90 seconds)
A compelling narrative or personal anecdote with a clear beginning, middle, and end.

**Example**: Origin stories, failure stories, transformation stories

### 5. The Guest Introduction (20-30 seconds)
The most impressive credential or achievement of your guest, creating authority and intrigue.

**Example**: *"My guest today has built and sold three companies for over $100M..."*

## Technical Execution: What Actually Matters

> **"Perfect is the enemy of posted."**

You don't need a production studio. You need:

### Video Recording
* **Minimum**: Record yourself via Zoom/Riverside with video on
* **Better**: Simple webcam or phone camera setup
* **Best**: Basic two-camera setup for dynamic shots

### Visual Elements
* **Subtitles**: Non-negotiable. 85% of social video is watched without sound
* **Lower thirds**: Guest name and credentials  
* **Progress bars**: Visual interest for longer clips
* **B-roll**: Optional but powerful when relevant

### Aspect Ratios
* **9:16 vertical**: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
* **1:1 square**: Instagram feed, LinkedIn, Twitter
* **16:9 horizontal**: YouTube, website embeds

The good news? Modern AI tools can handle all of this automatically, including identifying the best moments and reformatting for different platforms.

## Platform-Specific Strategies

### YouTube Shorts
* **Advantage**: Drives subscribers to your full podcast  
* **Best practice**: End with "Full episode in description"
* **Frequency**: 2-3 clips per episode

### Instagram Reels
* **Advantage**: Builds a visual brand and community
* **Best practice**: Consistent visual style and branding
* **Frequency**: Daily posting for maximum reach

### TikTok
* **Advantage**: Unmatched discovery potential
* **Best practice**: Embrace platform culture and trends
* **Frequency**: 1-2 clips per day

### LinkedIn
* **Advantage**: B2B audience and business focus
* **Best practice**: Educational, thought leadership content
* **Frequency**: 3-5 clips per week

### Twitter/X
* **Advantage**: Real-time engagement and conversation
* **Best practice**: Thread clips together for context
* **Frequency**: 5-7 clips per week

## The Workflow Problem (And Solution)

Here's the painful truth: manually creating clips can take 3-4 hours per episode. Most podcasters try it once, get overwhelmed, and give up.

The solution isn't working harder—it's working smarter.

### The Old Way
1. Listen through entire episode
2. Manually note timestamps
3. Export clips in editing software
4. Add subtitles manually
5. Reformat for each platform
6. Upload everywhere individually

**Total time**: 3-4 hours per episode

### The Smart Way
1. Upload episode to AI-powered tool
2. Review AI-identified clips
3. Approve and customize
4. Auto-generate formatted versions
5. Schedule across platforms

**Total time**: 15-20 minutes per episode

That's not a minor improvement. That's the difference between sustainable and impossible.
